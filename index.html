Idaho Climate & Coatings

Painted Right for the High Desert

Southeast Idaho's climate is hard on paint. Elevation above 4,000 ft means intense UV degradation year-round. Winters drop well below freezing. Summers push 100°F+ on south-facing metal and masonry surfaces. Dry air creates rapid flash-off that traps solvent or causes pinholes in improperly applied heavy coatings.

We don't apply paint when substrate temperatures are below 50°F, when surfaces are within 5°F of the dew point, or when forecast conditions will close the cure window. That discipline is what separates a lasting coating from one that peels in two seasons.

  • ✓ Application timed to air and surface temperature windows
  • ✓ Dew-point monitoring before and during exterior application
  • ✓ UV-stable exterior finishes rated for high-altitude sun exposure
  • ✓ Cold-weather application experience for tight project schedules
  • ✓ Surface temp checks on metal and masonry before every coat
